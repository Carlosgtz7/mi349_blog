The Rise, Fall, and Rise Again of My Fantasy Football Team

September 23, 2025 | By Carlos Gutierrez

This year is my second time playing fantasy football. Last year I was in a 12 man league with my buddies and managed to have the best record in the league (12-2) despite hardly knowing anything about football. This year I was hoping to keep that momentum going. On draft night, positions were revealed and I had the first overall pick. Things were really looking good, especially being a 14 man league this year having the first pick becomes significantly more valuable. Overall I would say drafting went pretty well, I had some heavy hitters on my team such as Joe Burrow and Jamarr Chase with plenty of other solid players.

Fast forward to the week 1 of the season and things already took a turn. I took an unfortunate loss as many of my starters had horrible performances. Nothing to panic over of course, I lost week 1 last year and still finished 12-2 after all. It was week 2 where the team really crumbled before my eyes. Three of my starting players went down with serious injuries this week, one of them being Bengals quarterback Joe Burrow. This was the most devastating loss as without him the Bengals offense is significantly worse and I just so happen to have two more Bengals players on my roster.

At this point my team is in absolute shambles, so it was time to make some trades. In short, I made three trades where I essentially gave up Jamarr Chase, Kareem Hunt, Chris Godwin, and Bryce Young in exchange for Bijan Robinson, Tyreek Hill, Jameson Williams, Trevor Lawrence, and Bo Nix. To put into perspective how good these two trades were for my team I put together this chart below.

Trade Value Chart

How I Broke 80 for the First Time (and How Long it Took)

September 20, 2025 | By Carlos Gutierrez

Breaking 80 is a mark many golfers use as sort of a gold standard for what you could consider a good player. For those that don’t know, the term “breaking 80” is defined by shooting a score of 79 or lower through 18 holes. According to CleverGolfer.com, roughly 2-5% of golfers ever break 80. It is a challenging goal that takes a significant amount of time and practice to achieve. With that being said, it’s not an impossible to goal to reach and in fact takes much less perfection than people realize. This post is going to detail the skillset I used to break 80 for the first time (and many more times after that).

Often people think that you need to play flawless golf in order to shoot under 80, but this is not the case. A huge standard you need to hold yourself to when attempting to break 80 is to eliminate double bogey. If you have two double bogeys on your scorecard, chances are you won’t get two birdies to make up for that, and your chances of breaking 80 are gone. As far as actually hitting your clubs, I would say the most important parts are hitting fairly straight irons and having a solid chipping game. I’ve managed to straighten out my driver a bit over the years but it’s my iron game that really saves the day. As long as I can give myself a shot at the green with an iron, I’m good. With that being said I am by no means hitting every green in regulation, that’s why I think chipping might be the most important part to breaking 80 (for my game at least). As far as putting goes, I was able to break 80 with just ok putting, nothing special. One last thing I’ll say is crucial to have in your game is the ability to get out of sticky situations and playing within your abilities.

Breaking 80 is an illusive goal to achieve but does not require anything close to perfect golf. As is the case with me, you can break 80 by having just two good parts of your game while the rest is just ok.
